Overview

A concept for a simple note-taking application inspired by Dieter Rams’ ‘less but better’ approach.  All the elements of this product have a specific and minimalistic function. The app allows to edit notes and group them into simple lists.

Purpose and context

This project was a part of my UI design certification from Career Foundry school. The specification for this product was simple - build a clean interface with minimal requirements. All of that based on one of Dieter Rams’ quote, which you can find at the end of this project.

In order to execute this project I wanted to find a minimalistic and elegant language, that would help the user to concentrate on a simple task of writing down various thoughts in the form of notes. I started to search for inspirations loosely connected with imagination and creativity.

Objectives

1
Dieter Rams' rules
Learning about the background of the designer and reading the ’10 principles of good design’.
2
Searching for the function
Drawing low-fidelity wireframes for 5 of my screens and eliminating unnecessary elements.
3
Finding the style
Letting the visual language to be defined by the necessary functions of the application.

Moodboard

Once I defined all the most important functions of the product I decided to search for my visual inspirations. Imagination and creativity were the topics that I concentrated on. I wanted to give my product a dreamy and phantasmagoric feeling which I wanted to achieve with gradients.

Branding

Light and minimalistic. The visual language of Words is based on gradients and two types of fonts. Non-disruptive design helping to concentrate on writing down the notes.

Learnings and takeaways

I have to admit that this project is probably one of the most minimalistic executions that I have worked on until this moment. I’ve noticed that this approach not only allows to concentrate on the functionality but also puts a heavier accent on basic elements such as typography, grids and white space. I believe this approach requires a lot of self-esteem and awareness from the designer.
