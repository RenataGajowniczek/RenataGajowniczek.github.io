Overview

A concept for ingredients based mobile application that allows the users to save and search for the recipes. From daily recipe recommendations to grocery list, Kitchen Beats offers various tools and information to make cooking process simple.

Purpose and context

How many times do we hear that humans are lazy by nature? It’s a question that bothered me for a while and something that I wanted to challenge. I decided to create an app for people, who are interested in learning new recipes.

My hypothesis was, that if we remove the obstacles by a possibility of ordering unknown ingredients and giving a chance to learn something about a foreign culture, we might motivate users to do something more than casual cooking or preparing food from only the ingredients that they already.

Remove waste

After the first round of interviews and creation of personas I eliminated all the hypothesis that don’t serve my users. I decided to pursue with learning aspect of my application and concentrated on ingredients, which played essential role for all interviewed by me people.

Ideation

With help of user stories I started to create user flows and wireframes. This helped me to visualise specific actions that users would accomplish in the application. It allowed me also to create a proper structure and save some time before starting more visually oriented design.

Moodboard

Once I had the structure of the application in place I started my search for the visual language. To help in visualising my thoughts I created the moodboard with illustration based style. I find it as a best way to communicate with the client in order to avoid misunderstandings and manage expectations.

Branding

The visual language of Kitchen Beats is ment to be playful and fresh through illustrations, vivid colours and rounded icons. White space and serif font add it some extra elagance.

What didn't go well

During the first interviews I had difficulties with making my testers say out loud what they think. In order to make this process more efficient I started to provide scenarios instead of dry instructions. Another problem that I observed was that not all tested by me users fitted the target audience of the product, which made me interview few additional people.

Learnings and takeaways

This was my first project where I was able to conduct the interviews and understand the ‘why’ behind the product. As a beginner I fell into a trap of creating ambitious hypothesis which were difficult to prove or to develop further. Over the time I’ve learnt how to ask questions and observe the user’s goals and needs instead of projecting my subjective opinions.
