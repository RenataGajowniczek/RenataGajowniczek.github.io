Overview

Concept design for a set of vinyl packages for indie rock bands. The project includes The Strokes, Kasabian, The Bright Eyes, Band of Horses and The Black Keys. It’s probably the biggest folding challenge that I had to face until now as a designer.

Purpose and context

This project is one of the oldest designs that I keep in my portfolio. I have a huge sentiment to it because it was the final task during my studies. It’s also the biggest print challenge that I had to face as a designer. I had to spend a lot of time preparing designs and cardboard prototypes.

Everything started from a simple idea of designing a packaging set for five bands. The challenge was to make a coherent series of designs with keeping the unique features of layouts for each band.

Objectives

1
Finding the motive
Grid system with geometric elements seemed like an interesting motive for 2D and 3D aspects of the packaging.
2
Building a coherent brand
Easy to multiply and diversify elements were key to this project. It’s quite a challenge to make five designs unique.
3
Testing paper prototypes
Before ordering professional prints I had to create 1:1 cardboard prototypes with the complicated folding system.

Moodboard

My research was concentrated on grids and geometrical shapes as the main features of my designs. I had to be sure that I can create enough shapes to diversify the final products. Since the packagings would also serve a marketing purpose I wanted to include in it the images of the artists.

Branding

The final design of Indie Rock collection is based on the grid system, where each band is represented by a unique set of modules, colours, images of artists and typographical logos. All of the inner packagings are meant to stand in contrast to calm and monochromatic outer packaging.

Learnings and takeaways

Indie Rock collection is one of those projects that gives an enormous satisfaction simply because the end product is an actual three-dimensional object. Designing 1:1 prototypes from cardboard was a huge lesson of precision and patience. It taught me how to arrange space and how to think about the composition. It was also a great exercise in building a diverse and coherent branding.
