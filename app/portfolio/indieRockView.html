Overview

Concept design for set of vinyl packages for indie rock bands. Project includes: The Strokes, Kasabian, The Bright Eyes, Band of Horses and The Black Keys. It’s probably the biggest folding challenge that I had to face until now as a designer.

Purpose and context

This project is one of the oldest designs that I keep in my portfolio. I have a huge sentiment to it because it was the final task during my studies. It’s also the biggest print challange that I had to face as a designer. I had to spent a lot of time preparing designs and cardboard prototypes.

Everything started from a simple idea of designing a packaging set for five bands. The challenge was to make a coherent series of designs with keeping the unique features of layouts for each band.

Objectives

1
Finding the motiv
Grid system with geometric elements seemed like an interesting motive for 2D and 3D aspects of the packaging.
2
Building a coherent brand
Easy to multiply and diversify elements were a key to this project. It’s quite a challenge to make five designs unique.
3
Testing paper prototypes
Before ordering profesional prints I had to create 1:1 cardboard prototypes with complicated foldings.

Moodboard

My research was concentrated on grids and geometrical shapes as a main features of my designs. I had to be sure that I can create enough shapes to diversify the final products. Since the packagings would also serve a marketing purpose I wanted to include in the layouts the images of the artists.

Branding

The final design of Indie Rock collection is based on the grid system, where each band is represented by unique set of modules, colours, images of artists and typographical logos. All of the inner packagings are meant to stand in contrast to calm and monochromatic outer packaging.

Learnings and takeaways

Finding the right colour scheme was probably the biggest challenge and learning in this project. Most of the elements in this branding are based on 3 primary colours and typical golden ratio proportion (6:3:1). Where dark blue is a dominant colour, read is an accent for all call to actions and grey remains neutral. Secondary colours present in this branding are reserved for illustrations and tags.
