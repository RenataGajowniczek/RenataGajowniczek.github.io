Overview

Collection of various sketches and paintings prepared fully with digital tools. Until now I was interested in such a topics as people, animals, urban spaces and environments. However, I’m always searching for new topics and inspirations to expand my skills.

Tools
Wacom Intuos Pro
Photoshop
Team
Individual

Objectives

1
Finding references
Searching for other illustrations or photography that already covered researched by me topic. Deciding about visual direction.
2
Studing the topic
Sketching the objects in order to understand their characteristics such as form, proportions, texture or movement.
3
Preparing for final illustration
Drawing thumbnails, collecting colouor moodboords, writing down the story that stands behind the character or scene

First sketches

This part is very vital for the whole process. I use it mainly to study chosen by me topics and to expand the visual library that I’m storing in my memory. I might use it to learn how to draw new objects or to research various compositions, characters or stories.

Final illustration

Once I’m done with all my preparations I’m moving on to the final stage of painting my illustrations. I tend to remove all the outlines and keep a painterly style that tries to emulate traditional techniques. I prepare some of the brushes myself by scanning water colour or linocut textures.

Learnings and takeaways

Until recently I used my digital painting skills to copy images or to create new scenes with help of references. The older I am the more I realise how powerful design thinking is and that I can use it also in my paintings. My goal is to introduce story telling into my environments and design thought through characters. Hopefully, I can develop a style that would also fit well into my webdesign projects.
