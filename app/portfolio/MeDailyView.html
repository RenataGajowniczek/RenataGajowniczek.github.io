Overview

A concept for a to-do list application that simplifies management of tracking daily routines. From planning a call with the best friend to creating a learning routine for German language. Me Daily helps to keep under control all of the simple activities.

Software
Sketch, Invision
Team
Individual
Duration
3 weeks

Purpose and context

To-do lists are a quite popular type of applications available on the market. To understand them better I decided to analyse few currently available products. Following users feedback I chose to concentrate on calendar, categories, reminders and editing options for single task.

From the visual perspective I observed that most of the analysed by me apps where quite subtle when it comes to colour choices. I decided to experiment with multi-coloured branding, which could be both useful for the user and making the branding stand out.

Objectives

1
Competitor analysis
Researching strengths and weaknesses of the most popular to-do list apps that are currently available.
2
Building hierarchy
Understanding the most important functionalities of the design and making them stand out.
3
Evoking emotions
Bringing vivid colours, organic shapes and illustrations to create a personality of the brand.

Moodboard

I concentrated my research on visuals full of playfulness, vivid colours and organic shapes. Since Me Daily doesn’t require any photographic elements I decided to tell the story of the brand with illustration based moodboard. I consider it the best way of managing expectations at early stages of branding.

Branding

Learnings and takeaways

Finding the right colour scheme was probably the biggest challenge and learning in this project. Most of the elements in this branding are based on 3 primary colours and typical golden ratio proportion (6:3:1). Where dark blue is a dominant colour, read is an accent for all call to actions and grey remains neutral. Secondary colours present in this branding are reserved for illustrations and tags.
