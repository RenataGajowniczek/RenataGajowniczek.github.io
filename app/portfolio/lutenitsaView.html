Overview

Lutenitsa is a name of a traditional vegetable dish typical for Bulgarian cuisine. A few years ago this product appeared on a Swedish market and I had a pleasure to work on the visual branding as well as the design and implementation of the home page.

Purpose and context

Misho & Maja are a Bulgarian-Swedish couple based in Stockholm and from them starts the history of this project. During one of their trips to Bulgaria, the homeland of Misho, Maja fell in love with Lutenitsa and together with her partner decided to bring this dish on a Swedish market.

Since both of them took this project very seriously they decided to ask for professional help of the design studio, where I was working at that time. That’s how started my tasty journey with Bulgarian Lutenitsa.

Moodboard

I concentrated my research on a visual language related to traditional folk patterns, modern typography and clean layouts with a lot of white space. I had a feeling that a mix of traditional and contemporary elements can give an interesting final result.

Branding

The visual language of Lutenitsa is a frame for telling a story of a sunny day, where friends and family are meeting together to enjoy the meal.

Learnings and takeaways

I will always remember this project as a very refreshing and fun task, where my old team had a lot of place to release its creativity. What might come as a surprise is a technical part, that was the biggest challenge. All the labels were not only designed to attract the clients but also to provide them with all the mandatory info about the ingredients, bar code and best-before date. All of that distributed on a very small space.
