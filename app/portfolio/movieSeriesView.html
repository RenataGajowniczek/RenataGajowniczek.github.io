Overview

‘Kino Grozy’ translated from polish means simply horror movies. It’s a packaging concept for such a movies as: Wrong Turn, Hills Have Eyes, House of Wax. Project includes outer packaging and inner packagings to store all three movies.

Photoshop

Purpose and context

I believe that it won’t be a lie, if I say that ‘Kino Grozy’ is an artistic attempt to run away from stereotypical way of showing horror movies. If we decide to search for some inspirations related with this move genre we will mostly find layouts full of blood, torture weapons or screens from the movie itself.

To run away from stereotypes I decided to concentrate on place of action for each of the movies and depict them through simplified textures. To make the form of packaging more interesting I searched for foldings typical for brochures and artistic books.

Objectives

1
Finding inspiration
Packaging’s textures refer to forest, desert and an old museum, where action takes place in each of the movies.
2
Working with paper
In order to find an interesting and practical form for the packagings I had to work first with paper mockups.
3
Combining 2D and 3D
I tried to difersify the space of paper by dividing it into different sections of text, textures and plain areas.

Moodboard

My research was divided on two phases. One of them was concentrated on various textures resembling the place of action from movies. Another one was related with brochures and artistic books. All kind of leporello foldings were a source of very interesting inspirations.

Learnings and takeaways

Finding the right colour scheme was probably the biggest challenge and learning in this project. Most of the elements in this branding are based on 3 primary colours and typical golden ratio proportion (6:3:1). Where dark blue is a dominant colour, read is an accent for all call to actions and grey remains neutral. Secondary colours present in this branding are reserved for illustrations and tags.
