Overview

‘Kino Grozy’ translated from polish means simply horror movies. It’s a name for a packaging concept presented on this page. The design includes outer packaging and inner packaging for such movies as Wrong Turn, Hills Have Eyes and House of Wax.

Purpose and context

I believe that it won’t be a lie if I say that ‘Kino Grozy’ is an artistic attempt to run away from a stereotypical way of showing horror movies. If we decide to search for some inspirations related to this movie genre we will mostly find layouts full of blood, torture weapons or screens from the movie itself.

To run away from stereotypes I decided to concentrate on the place of action for each of the movies and depict them through simplified textures. To make the form of packaging more interesting I searched for foldings typical for brochures and artistic books.

Objectives

1
Finding inspiration
Packagings' textures refer to a forest, desert and an old museum, where the action takes place.
2
Working with paper
In order to find an interesting and practical form, I had to work first with paper mockups.
3
Combining 2D and 3D
I tried to diversify the space by dividing it into different sections of text, textures and plain areas.

Moodboard

My research was divided into three phases. In the first one, I concentrated on old horror movies and black and white posters. In the second phase, I was researching various textures resembling the place of action from movies. In the final phase, I concentrated on brochures and artistic books. All kind of leporello foldings were a source of very interesting inspirations.

Learnings and takeaways

Being able to create designs that later turn into actual objects gives some extra satisfaction in the whole process of creation. Designing paper prototypes taught me a lot how to think about the space and how to arrange it. Although as a designer I’m not working on too many paper projects. I believe this one was a good exercise in evoking emotions, building hierarchy and working on the composition.
