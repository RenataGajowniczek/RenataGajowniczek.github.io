Overview

Nope! is a concept for a fashion brand dedicated for teenagers and young women. It’s main purpose is to promote young designers and produce fair fashion. As a talent incubator Nope! supports emerging talents in their first steps of advertising themselves.
Software
Sketch, Invision

Purpose and context

Nope! is a responsive web application that offers its products to women in standard categories such as: dresses, shorts, jackets, t-shirts, trausers etc. Each of the products is available in different sizes and colours. The general interface corresponds with currently available fashion websites. It’s intuitive and easy to learn for the user.
What makes this website stand out is a grid system and simple geometrical elements combined with the photos of the presented products.

Objectives

1
Competitor analysis
Researching strengths and weaknesses of the most popular fashion brands that are currently available.
2
Layout system
Creating a grid system that allows to present products in efficient and attractive ways to the users of the website.
3
Coherent visual language
Creating a believable experience that is consistent across all the visual elements of the brand.

Moodboard

Nope! concentrates on casual fashion and big number of products under various categories. I decided to use it as my advantage and search for grid based inspirations. Geometric shapes dividing further the layout seemed like a natural next step. Liveliness of vivid colours played also a big part.

Branding

The visual language of Nope! is based on simple geometric shapes, grid layout, one element fonts and playful colours contrasted with white space.

Learnings and takeaways

Products presented under the label Nope! can be categorised as a casual fashion. Their main value is hidden in small details, quality and fair production. The biggest challenge was to create a visual language that would stand out from the competition with its own character, but at the same time not overpower the presented products.
